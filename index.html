Extreme Weather Prediction | GNSS and Remote Sensing | Space Weather Researcher

I am a dedicated Ph.D. Research Scholar at the prestigious Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Indore, specializing in the interdisciplinary domains of Extreme Weather Prediction, Global Navigation Satellite System (GNSS) Applications, and Ionospheric Sciences within the broader framework of Astronomy, Astrophysics, and Space Engineering. My research intricately explores the dynamic behavior of the Earth's upper atmosphere, with a specific focus on the ionosphere, leveraging high-precision GNSS datasets, ionospheric modeling, and advanced computational techniques. By investigating ionospheric irregularities - such as Total Electron Content (TEC) fluctuations, scintillations, and traveling ionospheric disturbances (TIDs) - and their coupling with geospace and neutral atmospheric processes, I aim to enhance our understanding of space weather impacts and improve the reliability of satellite-based communication and navigation systems.

With a strong academic foundation and a passion for exploring the unknown, I strive to contribute to the expanding frontiers of space science through rigorous analysis, publication, and collaboration. My journey is driven by curiosity, precision, and a deep-seated commitment to knowledge that transcends boundaries.

Research Interests

  • Global Navigation Satellite System and Signal Processing
  • Extreme Weather Events
  • Satellite and Ground-Based Observational Techniques
